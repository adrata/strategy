VP Sales

Demand Hypotheses

What They're Already Trying to Do — Unsuccessfully | PULL Framework Analysis | January 2026

THE CORE MODEL: DEMAND VS. SUPPLY

KEY INSIGHT: Nobody wants your product. They want to accomplish something in their life. Your product is supply for their demand. Demand is supply-agnostic—it exists whether or not your product exists.

THE TENSION THAT CREATES PULL:
• Calendar: What they're actually doing (current supply)
• To-Do List: What they really want to accomplish (demand)
• The Gap: When calendar doesn't fit to-do list = PULL exists

Why Now: The 2026 Moment

Why would VP Sales buy NOW when they haven't before?

FORCE 1: THE HEADCOUNT SQUEEZE

What changed: Boards now expect AI to replace headcount, not augment it. Same quota, 30% fewer people.

Why they buy: Any tool that multiplies output of existing people is now essential.

FORCE 2: THE AI SDR FLOOD

What changed: Everyone adopted AI SDRs. Buyer inboxes 80% AI spam. Response rates down 60%.

Why they buy: Tools that cut through noise or work existing pipeline are more valuable than ever.

FORCE 3: THE "DIY WITH AI" COMPETITOR

What changed: ChatGPT taught everyone they can build things themselves. Every prospect considers "we'll just build it."

Why they buy: VP Sales themselves are hitting walls with DIY. Makes them receptive to specialized solutions.

FORCE 4: THE SPEED EXPECTATION

What changed: Headlines about $0-$20M in months reset board expectations.

Why they buy: Anything that accelerates time-to-revenue is worth paying for.

FORCE 5: THE TENURE CRISIS

What changed: VP Sales tenure dropped from 26 months (2010) to 17-19 months (2025).

Why they buy: Tools that show fast ROI (90 days or less) will get budget. Their jobs depend on it.

Demand Hypothesis #1

"I need to know what's real in my pipeline without relying on rep optimism."

THE DEMAND (To-Do List)

"I want to look at my pipeline and instantly know which deals are real and which are wishful thinking—without having to interrogate every rep. I want the truth about what's going to close, before I find out the hard way."

CURRENT SUPPLY (What They're Doing Now)

Supply A: Trust the CRM

Limitation: CRM reflects what reps input, not reality. VP spends hours trying to find truth.

Supply B: Weekly Deal Reviews

Limitation: Only covers subset. Takes 3-5 hours/week. Still gets surprised.

Supply C: DIY with ChatGPT

Limitation: Can't access real-time deal data. No integration. VP tries once, gives up.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP spends 5-10 hours/week trying to figure out what's real but still doesn't have confidence.
Ideal supply: Single source of truth that scores every deal's "realness" based on signals they can't see manually.
"I need to see deals slipping before they slip."

THE DEMAND

"I want to know a deal is at risk BEFORE it misses its close date—not after. I want early warning so I can intervene."

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP learns about deal risk reactively, not proactively. By the time it's visible, it's too late.
Ideal supply: Automated early warning system that detects slip signals 30-60 days before the deal misses.
"I need to know who's really involved in buying decisions."

THE DEMAND

"I want to see the FULL buying committee—including people who have influence but never show up in meetings. The IT person who will veto. The CFO's analyst who will kill the deal on price."

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP and reps can't see who they're NOT talking to. Hidden stakeholders are invisible until they veto.
Ideal supply: AI that detects likely buying committee members from email patterns, meeting invites, org data.
"I need to present pipeline to the board without looking uncertain."

THE DEMAND

"I want to walk into a board meeting with confidence that my forecast is right. I want the board to trust my numbers—because I trust my numbers."

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP can't confidently forecast because they don't have a reliable signal for deal health.
Ideal supply: AI-powered forecast that weighs deal-specific health signals, not just stage and close date.

THE THESIS: WHY NOW, WHY US

VP Sales have always wanted to see the truth about their pipeline. Their existing supply (CRM data, deal reviews, gut feel) has always been inadequate.

But in 2026, three things have changed:

  1. THE DATA EXISTS: 5 years of call recordings, emails, CRM history.
  2. THE AI CAN INTERPRET IT: LLMs can synthesize unstructured data into insight.
  3. THE PRESSURE IS EXISTENTIAL: 17-month tenure, smaller teams, broken outbound.

The demand has always existed. The supply has never been good enough. AI has changed the supply equation. This is the moment.
