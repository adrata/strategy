Interest-Building Framework

5 Proven Approaches to Spark Curiosity & Build Momentum

Every prospect is different. Some respond to pain, others to vision, and some need proof before they'll engage. This framework gives you 5 different entry points to build interest based on where your prospect is mentally.

Approach 1: Problem-First (Pain-Based Entry)

When to use: Prospect mentions challenges, seems frustrated, or you know their pain points
The Formula: Acknowledge → Agitate → Amplify

Opening Lines That Work

"Most sales leaders we talk to are drowning in data but starving for insights. How much time does your team spend in spreadsheets each week?"
"I'm curious - when a deal goes dark 30 days before quarter end, what's your backup plan?"
"You know that sinking feeling when your forecast is off by 40% and the board is asking questions? Yeah, we built this specifically for that."

The Conversation Flow

  • Start with their pain (they'll nod along)
  • Quantify the cost ("How much does a missed forecast cost you?")
  • Paint the alternative ("What if that never happened again?")

Power Phrases

"Let me guess..." "The worst part is..." "Here's what most people don't realize..."

Approach 2: Vision-First (Possibility-Based Entry)

When to use: Prospect is growth-minded, forward-thinking, or mentions scaling
The Formula: Paint the Future → Show the Gap → Bridge It

Opening Lines That Work

"Imagine closing 50% more deals next quarter without hiring a single person. That's what happens when your CRM actually works for you."
"What if your team could handle 3x the volume without the chaos? That's not a fantasy - three of your competitors are already doing it."
"Picture this: It's Friday at 4:55pm. Your CEO asks for a pipeline update. Instead of panic, you click one button and it's done."

Power Phrases

"Imagine if..." "What would it mean for your business if..." "The top performers in your industry are already..."

Approach 3: Proof-First (Evidence-Based Entry)

When to use: Skeptical prospects, analytical types, or when you have strong case studies
The Formula: Share Results → Show Similarity → Offer Same Outcome

Opening Lines That Work

"Last month, a VP of Sales went from 12 hours/week on reporting to 20 minutes. Want to know how?"
"We just helped a company your size increase win rates by 34% while cutting sales cycle by half. Curious why?"
"Three companies in your space are using this. They're closing deals 2 weeks faster on average."

Power Phrases

"We just helped..." "They were skeptical too, until..." "The numbers don't lie..."

Approach 4: Question-First (Discovery-Based Entry)

When to use: Building relationships, networking events, or when you need to qualify
The Formula: Ask → Listen → Reflect → Offer

Opening Lines That Work

"Quick question - how are you currently handling pipeline visibility when you have 50+ deals in play?"
"I'm curious about your process - when a rep goes quiet on a deal, who's responsible for catching it?"
"Walk me through what happens when a buyer ghosts you mid-cycle. How long does that typically take to recover?"

Power Questions

"What's the most frustrating part of..." "How much time does your team spend on..." "If you could wave a magic wand..."

Approach 5: Story-First (Narrative-Based Entry)

When to use: Building rapport, casual conversations, or when you need to be memorable
The Formula: Relatable Scenario → Crisis Moment → Resolution → Invitation

Opening Lines That Work

"Let me tell you about Sarah. She's a VP of Sales who was manually tracking 47 deals in a spreadsheet. One Friday, her biggest deal went silent. She spent 4 hours hunting for context. Now? She gets an alert the moment engagement drops."
"I was talking to a CRO last week. She told me she almost lost her best AE because the admin burden was too much. Three months after implementing our system, that AE is now training two new hires."

Story Elements That Hook

  • Specific details (47 deals, 4 hours, Friday at 3pm)
  • Emotional moments (panic, relief, transformation)
  • Clear before/after (chaos → calm)

Choosing Your Approach

Prospect Type Best Approach Why
Frustrated/Stressed Problem-First They're already feeling the pain
Growth-Oriented Vision-First They think in possibilities
Analytical/Skeptical Proof-First They need evidence
Curious/Open Question-First They'll tell you what they need
Relationship-Builder Story-First They connect through narratives

Pre-Conversation Checklist

  • Do I know which approach fits this prospect?
  • Have I prepared 2-3 opening lines?
  • Do I have a specific story/stat/question ready?
  • Can I pivot if this approach doesn't land?
  • Am I ready to listen more than I talk?

Remember: The goal isn't to pitch - it's to build enough interest that they ASK you to tell them more.
