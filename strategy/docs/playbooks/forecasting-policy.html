Forecasting Policy

How we talk about deals, what data we need, and what conviction means

The Goal of Forecasting

We forecast to make decisions, not to create busywork. Every field we ask for should help us understand: Should we invest more time here? When will this close? What's blocking it?

Conviction Levels

We use plain language to describe deal likelihood. No percentages. Words that mean the same thing to everyone.

Bankable

It's happening

Verbal commit, signed, or so close we'd bet on it. Champion is bought in, budget is approved, timeline is clear.

"They've already started onboarding. Just waiting on signature."
Guaranteed

100% if conditions are met

Deal will definitely close if a specific condition is true. Often used for conditional deals (e.g., "if we do this for free, they're in").

"If free: guaranteed. If $15K: low."
Average

Good momentum, growing conviction

Champion engaged, product fit is there, but still working through process. Could close this quarter with the right push.

"Phil loved the report and wants to talk more. Conviction growing."
Toss Up

Could go either way

Champion sees value but there's friction. Internal pushback, competing priorities, or unresolved objections. This is a LOW confidence deal.

"Dennis liked it but another rep pushed back. Coordinating next call."
Low

Not likely right now

Early stage, stalled, or significant blockers. Worth tracking but don't count on it. Needs a breakthrough to move forward.

"Introduced to CEO but haven't met yet. Former contact left the company."

Required Data Per Deal

Every deal in the pipeline needs these fields. No exceptions. If you can't fill a field, that tells us something about the deal.

Field What It Is Required
Company Name The account name Yes
Deal Value Upfront + recurring. Use ranges if uncertain ($40-60K) Yes
Cash Collected Money already in the bank from this deal Yes
Conviction Bankable, Guaranteed, Average, Toss Up, or Low Yes
Reason to Buy Why do they need this? What problem are we solving? Yes
Champion Who is your internal advocate? Recommended
Notes Context, blockers, next steps, anything that explains the conviction level Recommended

Example: Well-Documented Deal

Navi Nurses
$20K + $4K/mo
Cash Collected $24K
Conviction Bankable
Reason to Buy Need database for Ideal Navi Patient, need Speedrun CRM, and need Campaign (Adrata Marketing platform)

Example: Conditional Deal

Cardinal Gray
$0 - $15K/yr
Cash Collected $0
Conviction If free: Guaranteed | If $5K-$15K: Low
Reason to Buy 15 target accounts, no enterprise sales experience, needs buyer groups for net new and expansion plays
Notes Likely $0 in exchange for YC intros and qualified self-onboard feedback

Prospects vs. Deals

A Prospect is a company we're interested in but haven't qualified yet. No pipeline value, no conviction rating. Just a name on a list.

A Deal has a value, a conviction level, and a reason to buy. The moment you can articulate why they'd buy and what they'd pay, it becomes a deal.

The "Reason to Buy" Test

If you can't explain why they need this in one sentence, it's not a deal yet. "They seem interested" is not a reason to buy. "They're launching a new product and need Speedrun for their reps" is.
