Fast Pass

Some deals don't need the full process. When the decision-maker is on the first call and ready to buy, we skip directly to Close. Like bumping a critical bug to the top of the Jira backlog—some deals deserve priority treatment.

Fast Pass Criteria

  • Decision Authority: They can sign without approval
  • Budget Confirmed: "We have budget allocated"
  • Timeline Urgent: "We need this by [specific date]"
  • Problem Acute: Clear pain they've been trying to solve
  • Champion = Buyer: No internal selling required

Trigger phrase: "Can we just get started? I don't need to involve anyone else."

1

Offer Meeting

20 minutes • First contact

Purpose

Deliver on the promise that got them to book. Qualify ruthlessly. Create urgency through insight, not pressure.

Structure

  • 0-3 min: Rapport + confirm why they booked
  • 3-10 min: Deliver the offer (assessment, insight, value)
  • 10-15 min: Discovery—understand their specific situation
  • 15-20 min: Bridge to Strategy or disqualify

Psychology by Archetype

Desperate Closer Rising Star Steady Performer
  • Desperate Closer: Lead with urgency validation. "You're right to be worried. Here's what the data shows..." They need to feel understood, not pitched.
  • Rising Star: Frame as acceleration. "You're already outperforming. This is about 10x, not 2x." Feed their ambition.
  • Steady Performer: Lead with risk mitigation. "You're doing well. Let's make sure Q4 doesn't surprise you." Safety first.

Gate to Stage 2

  • Confirmed pain: They articulated a specific problem
  • Timeline exists: There's a reason this matters NOW
  • Budget plausible: Not a clear mismatch
  • Authority mapped: Know who else needs to be involved
  • Strategy meeting scheduled: Date on calendar
2

Strategy Meeting

45 minutes • Building the case

Purpose

Co-create the business case. Quantify the problem. Design what working together looks like. Make them the hero of their own transformation story.

Structure

  • 0-5 min: Recap and validate understanding
  • 5-20 min: Deep dive—current state, desired state, gap
  • 20-35 min: Solution mapping—how we bridge the gap together
  • 35-45 min: ROI framework and buyer group identification

Psychology by Archetype

Desperate Closer Rising Star Steady Performer
  • Desperate Closer: Focus on speed to value. "Here's how we get wins in week 1." They can't wait 6 months.
  • Rising Star: Show the career impact. "When this works, you become the executive who transformed sales." Feed the narrative.
  • Steady Performer: Emphasize de-risking. "We'll run this in parallel. No disruption to what's working." Safety blanket.

Gate to Stage 3

  • ROI quantified: Specific dollar impact articulated
  • Buyer group identified: Names, not roles
  • Champion committed: They'll internal sell
  • Objections surfaced: Know what blocks exist
  • Buyer group meeting scheduled: Date on calendar
3

Buyer Group Meeting

60 minutes • Expanding the room

Purpose

Align all stakeholders on value. Address each buyer's specific concerns. Turn the buyer group into advocates, not obstacles.

Structure

  • 0-10 min: Introductions + champion frames the opportunity
  • 10-25 min: Present the business case (ROI, timeline, risk)
  • 25-45 min: Address each stakeholder's concerns
  • 45-60 min: Align on next steps and proposal review

Psychology by Buyer Group Role

See Buyer Group Archetypes for deep profiles. Key approaches:

  • CFO/Finance: Lead with ROI and payback period. "Break-even in 90 days. 4x return in year one."
  • Legal/Procurement: Lead with compliance and risk. "SOC 2 certified. Standard MSA. No data exposure."
  • IT/Security: Lead with integration and security. "API-first. Your data stays in your environment."
  • Other VPs: Lead with collaboration. "This makes your team's job easier, not harder."

Gate to Stage 4

  • All stakeholders heard: No silent objectors
  • Concerns addressed: Nothing unresolved
  • Budget confirmed: Finance nodded
  • Timeline agreed: Everyone aligned on start date
  • Proposal requested: "Send us the numbers"
  • Negotiate meeting scheduled: Date on calendar
4

Negotiate Meeting

30 minutes • Proposal, pricing, terms

Purpose

Review the proposal. Finalize pricing and terms. Handle commercial objections. Get alignment on the path to signature.

Structure

  • 0-5 min: Confirm nothing has changed since buyer group
  • 5-15 min: Walk through proposal—scope, timeline, pricing
  • 15-25 min: Address pricing questions, negotiate terms if needed
  • 25-30 min: Align on next steps to signature

Negotiation Psychology

  • Anchor high: Start with your standard pricing. Give ground strategically, not reflexively.
  • Trade, don't cave: If they want discount, get something back (longer term, case study, referral).
  • Assume the deal: "When we kick off next Monday..." not "If you decide to move forward..."
  • Remove friction: Digital signature ready. No "send it over and I'll print it."
  • Handle the pause: Silence after asking for signature is fine. Don't fill it.

Gate to Stage 5

  • Terms agreed: Pricing and scope finalized
  • Signature obtained: Contract signed
  • Payment processed: Or payment terms confirmed
  • Kickoff scheduled: Implementation start date set
5

Won / Signed

Closed deal

Definition

Contract signed. Payment confirmed or terms agreed. Deal is closed-won. Handoff to implementation.

Immediate Actions

  • Send welcome package within 1 hour
  • Schedule kickoff call within 24 hours
  • Introduce customer success manager
  • Update CRM with full deal details
  • Celebrate internally (ring the bell)

Why This Matters

The experience immediately after signing sets the tone for the entire relationship. Speed and professionalism now creates advocates later.
