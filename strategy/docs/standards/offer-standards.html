Offer Standards

How to create offers that generate PULL. Reference for humans and AI agents.

Step 0: Which archetype? Every offer must align with the emotional driver of a specific archetype. Their enemy is the problem; the offer is the solution they can't get elsewhere. See Archetypes Persona.

The Offer Test: Can they do this themselves?

If yes, the offer won't create PULL. If no—if only we can deliver it at this speed and quality—they have a reason to engage.

The 4 Offer Principles

1. Align with Emotional Drivers

Every archetype has a core fear (enemy). The offer must address that specific fear, not a generic pain point.

  • Anxious Elite: Fear = Pipeline Exposure → Offer = visibility into what they don't see
  • Worried Darling: Fear = Golden Trap → Offer = protection without public admission
  • Stressed Performer: Fear = Silent Slip → Offer = ammunition for leadership

2. Don't Demand Unearned Trust

A stranger asking to "audit your pipeline" requires trust they haven't built.

Earned Trust

  • "Give us 5 accounts, we'll map the buying committees"
  • "We do the work—you keep it regardless"
  • "No CRM access. Just account names."

Unearned Trust

  • "Let us audit your pipeline"
  • "Connect your CRM for a health check"
  • "Share your deal data for analysis"

3. Don't Offer What They Can Do Internally

"We'll review your tech stack" → "I'll just have ops do that."

"We'll map buying committees with 90% accuracy in 48 hours" → They literally cannot do this.

4. Differentiation is Everything

The offer must leverage something only Adrata can do. Our differentiator: instantly map any buying committee with pain intelligence and personal archetypes for each stakeholder.

Offer Validation Test

Ask: Would a VP of Sales forward this to their CEO as proof they're doing something about the problem?

Would they tell a peer about it? If the offer creates something they'd share or use immediately, it works.
