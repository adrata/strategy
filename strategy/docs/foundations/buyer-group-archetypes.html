Buyer Group Archetypes

Psychology profiles for every stakeholder in the enterprise buying committee

Enterprise deals don't close because one person said yes. They close because no one said no. Each stakeholder has different fears, different goals, and different definitions of success. Master these archetypes to navigate any buying committee.

$

The CFO

Budget Holder • Decision Ratifier • ROI Gatekeeper
Primary Concern
Return on Investment
Decision Style
Data-driven, conservative
Influence Level
Can veto any deal
Meeting Preference
Short, numbers-focused

Fears

  • Wasted budget on shelfware
  • Looking foolish to the board
  • Hidden costs that balloon
  • Unquantifiable "soft" benefits

Wants

  • Clear payback period (ideally <6 months)
  • Measurable, defensible ROI
  • Predictable costs, no surprises
  • Reference customers in similar situations

Winning Talk Track

"Based on your current [metric], we project a [X]% improvement, translating to [$Y] in additional revenue over 12 months. With a [$Z] investment, that's a [N]x return. We break even at month [M]. Here's how three similar companies performed..."

L

The Legal Lead

Contract Reviewer • Risk Assessor • Liability Shield
Primary Concern
Contractual Risk
Decision Style
Cautious, thorough
Influence Level
Can delay indefinitely
Meeting Preference
Document-focused, async OK

Fears

  • Liability exposure
  • Non-standard terms they'll regret
  • Data breaches or compliance violations
  • Being blamed when things go wrong

Wants

  • Standard MSA with minimal redlines
  • Clear data handling and DPA
  • Reasonable limitation of liability
  • Quick turnaround on their questions

Winning Talk Track

"We use a standard enterprise MSA that's been reviewed by [X] Fortune 500 legal teams. Our DPA is GDPR and CCPA compliant. Here's our SOC 2 Type II report. Most customers complete legal review in 5-7 days. Happy to schedule a call with your team to address any specific concerns."

P

Procurement

Process Enforcer • Vendor Manager • Cost Optimizer
Primary Concern
Process Compliance
Decision Style
By-the-book, structured
Influence Level
Can force competitive bid
Meeting Preference
Formal, documented

Fears

  • Audit findings on process violations
  • Being bypassed or undermined
  • Paying more than they should
  • Vendor lock-in without leverage

Wants

  • Clear pricing with no hidden fees
  • Competitive quotes (even if pro forma)
  • Standard vendor onboarding flow
  • Multi-year discount for commitment

Winning Talk Track

"We're happy to go through your standard vendor qualification process. Here's our W-9, insurance certificates, and completed security questionnaire. Our pricing is transparent—no hidden fees. For multi-year commitments, we offer [X]% discount. What else do you need to keep this on track?"

S

IT Security

Technical Gatekeeper • Risk Analyst • Integration Owner
Primary Concern
Security & Integration Risk
Decision Style
Technical, skeptical
Influence Level
Hard veto on security grounds
Meeting Preference
Technical deep-dive

Fears

  • Data breaches they'll be blamed for
  • Shadow IT and uncontrolled access
  • Integration nightmares
  • Another tool to maintain and secure

Wants

  • SOC 2, ISO 27001 compliance
  • SSO integration (SAML/OIDC)
  • Clear data residency and encryption
  • API documentation and sandboxes

Winning Talk Track

"We're SOC 2 Type II certified, here's our report. Data is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). We support SAML SSO with your identity provider. All data stays in [region], no third-party processing. Here's our security whitepaper and API docs. Want to schedule a technical architecture review?"

V

Peer VP

Territory Protector • Political Player • Potential Ally
Primary Concern
Impact on Their World
Decision Style
Political, relationship-driven
Influence Level
Can rally opposition quietly
Meeting Preference
1:1 or small group

Fears

  • Their team being disrupted
  • Losing budget to another VP's initiative
  • Looking unsupportive if it succeeds
  • Being left out of credit if it works

Wants

  • To be consulted, not informed
  • Clear boundaries on scope
  • Shared credit for success
  • No additional work for their team

Winning Talk Track

"We wanted to get your input before moving forward. This is [Champion]'s initiative, but it will touch [your area]. Our goal is to make your team's job easier, not harder. What concerns would you want us to address? How can we make sure this is a win for your team too?"

C

The Champion

Internal Seller • Project Owner • Success Dependent
Primary Concern
Making This Work
Decision Style
Committed, needs ammunition
Influence Level
Drives the deal forward
Meeting Preference
Prep calls, strategy sessions

Fears

  • Staking reputation on failure
  • Losing momentum internally
  • Being unable to answer objections
  • You making them look bad

Wants

  • Win to accelerate their career
  • Ammunition for every objection
  • To be the hero of the story
  • Partner, not vendor relationship

Winning Talk Track

"You're going to be the one presenting this internally. Let's make sure you have everything you need. What objections are you anticipating? Who's the toughest stakeholder? Let me give you the exact data points that worked with [similar customer]. You're not alone in this—we'll prep you for every meeting."

The Golden Rule

Every stakeholder has a reason to say no and a reason to say yes. Your job is to find their specific reason to say yes and eliminate their specific reason to say no. Generic pitches hit neither.
