The 10 Archetypes

Our Archetypes for VP of Sales. Ranked by conversion potential. Each with psychology, language calibration, and hook strategy.

How to use this document: Every piece of content, every ad, every offer should target 1-2 specific archetypes. Use their language. Name their enemy. Match your tech framing to their adoption profile. Innovators want "AI Agents." Late majority wants "Enhanced CRM." Meet them where they are.

#1 Priority
Think of
Jon Hamm as Don Draper
The Anxious Elite
Enemy: Pipeline Exposure — what they don't see will destroy them
Hit their number, but pipeline is slipping. Has budget, credibility, and board visibility. Sophisticated, skeptical, suspects something is wrong but can't pinpoint it. Needs validation of gut instinct, not education. They already know — they need proof.
Tech Adoption
Innovator / Early Adopter
Urgency Level
Maximum — ego on the line
Language That Converts
AI Agents Autonomous Intelligence Buying Committee Mapping Deal Intelligence
Avoid: "CRM enhancement", "sales enablement", "pipeline management" — too basic, they'll tune out
"You hit your number. Your pipeline says you won't again. Here's what the top 1% do differently."
#2 Priority
Think of
Ryan Gosling in The Big Short
The Worried Darling
Enemy: Golden Trap — success that's actually fragile, one quarter from collapse
All the support in the world. CEO's favorite. But they know it's luck or timing, not system. Can't admit vulnerability publicly — would shatter the image. Needs private, confidential solutions. They become your secret weapon.
Tech Adoption
Early Adopter
Urgency Level
High — fear of embarrassment
Language That Converts
AI Sales Intelligence Predictive Deal Health Hidden Risk Detection Stealth Advantage
Avoid: Anything that sounds like "fixing a problem" — they can't publicly admit they have one
"Everyone expects you to win. Only you see the cracks. Stay untouchable."
#3 Priority
Think of
McConaughey in Wolf of Wall Street
The Stressed Performer
Enemy: Silent Slip — deals quietly dying while everyone focuses on new pipeline
Decent resources. Watching pipeline thin. Feels like running in sand — working harder but not gaining. Needs ammunition to justify resources to leadership. Wants proof they can take to their CEO.
Tech Adoption
Early Majority / Pragmatist
Urgency Level
High — stress building
Language That Converts
Buyer Intelligence Deal Visibility Pipeline Analytics Revenue Intelligence
Avoid: "AI Agents" (too futuristic), "disruption" — they want proven, not experimental
"Your pipeline doesn't lie. Q1 is coming. Act now or explain later."
#4
Think of
Miles Teller in Whiplash
The Stretched Striver
Enemy: Resource Gap — competing against teams 10x their size with 1/10th the budget
Marketing wants to help but budget is tight. Grinding harder than anyone, but system isn't scaling. Needs ROI-proven tactics, not experiments. Will try unconventional if it's cheap and proven.
Tech Adoption
Early Adopter — driven by necessity
Urgency Level
High — needs leverage
Language That Converts
Force Multiplier AI-Powered Efficiency Do More With Less Unfair Advantage
Avoid: Enterprise pricing language, "comprehensive platforms" — they can't afford it
"Tight budget? Tighter pipeline? Here's how underdogs generate demand without spend."
#5
Think of
Michael B. Jordan in Creed
The Rebuilding Leader
Enemy: Miss Pattern — inherited a broken machine, needs to prove previous regime was wrong
Missed Q4 but has mandate and resources. New in role or new regime. Wants to prove they're different. Looking for partners, not vendors. They WANT to spend to prove their approach works.
Tech Adoption
Innovator — wants to be first
Urgency Level
High — has mandate to act
Language That Converts
AI Agents Next-Gen Sales Strategic Partnership Transformation
Avoid: "Incremental improvement", "optimization" — they want revolution, not evolution
"Q4 was a miss. Q1 is your redemption. Here's the playbook for leaders who build different."
#6
Think of
Kevin Spacey in House of Cards
The Scrambling Politician
Enemy: Exposure — surviving on relationships, but results are slipping and cover is thinning
Got here through political savvy. Knows the right people. But numbers are starting to betray them. Needs a win — something to point to. Conservative buyer, wants safety over innovation.
Tech Adoption
Late Majority — needs social proof
Urgency Level
Medium-High — needs cover
Language That Converts
CRM Intelligence Enterprise Trusted Fortune 500 Proven Industry Standard
Avoid: "Cutting edge", "AI Agents", "disruptive" — too risky for their brand
"Politics got you here. Only pipeline keeps you here. The playbook top performers won't share."
#7
Think of
Alec Baldwin in Glengarry Glen Ross
The Frustrated Fighter
Enemy: Marketing Failure — has budget but marketing isn't delivering leads
Angry and motivated. Has resources but feels abandoned by marketing. Wants to build own pipeline engine. Distrusts vendors but respects results. Will buy if you prove you're different.
Tech Adoption
Innovator — willing to try anything
Urgency Level
High — frustrated into action
Language That Converts
Sales-Led Growth AI Agents for Sales Zero Marketing Dependency Direct Pipeline Generation
Avoid: "Marketing alignment", "funnel optimization" — they blame marketing
"Marketing isn't coming to save you. Here's how sales leaders build their own pipeline engine."
#8
Think of
Jonah Hill in Moneyball
The Hungry Underdog
Enemy: The Establishment — bigger competitors with 10x resources who underestimate them
Missed but building momentum with scrappy tactics. Chip on shoulder. Coachable, hungry. Will out-execute anyone if given the tools. Best long-term customer — loyal when you help them win.
Tech Adoption
Early Adopter — needs edge
Urgency Level
Medium — hungry but patient
Language That Converts
Unfair Advantage Moneyball for Sales Outperform the Big Guys Scrappy Intelligence
Avoid: Enterprise pricing, "comprehensive suite" — they need point solutions that punch above weight
"No budget. No problem. The scrappy sales leader's guide to outperforming teams 10x your size."
#9
Think of
Brad Pitt in Moneyball
The Recovering Performer
Enemy: The Miss — can't let it define them, must prove it was an aberration
Missed but has support and is rebuilding. Optimistic, open to new ideas. More coachable than successful peers. Wants proven frameworks they can execute. Lower ego, higher learning velocity.
Tech Adoption
Early Majority — open but careful
Urgency Level
Medium — rebuilding momentum
Language That Converts
Buyer Intelligence Proven Playbooks Revenue Intelligence Data-Driven Sales
Avoid: "Revolutionary", "cutting edge" — they want reliable, not risky
"You missed. Now you're building. Here's how to make Q1 your comeback story."
#10
Think of
Leonardo DiCaprio in Wolf of Wall Street
The Sweating Target
Enemy: The Clock — hit number but pipeline is dying and seat is at risk
Hit their number but pipeline is empty. Seat is at risk. Maximum urgency but may lack pull to buy quickly. Conservative by nature — wants safe, proven solutions. Short-term focused.
Tech Adoption
Late Majority — wants safety
Urgency Level
Maximum — survival mode
Language That Converts
Immediate Pipeline Quick Wins Proven Results Fast Implementation
Avoid: Long-term strategy, "transformation", "AI Agents" — they need results this quarter, not next year
"Your number won't save you if pipeline is empty. This is the quarter that matters."
