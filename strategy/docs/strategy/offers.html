Offer Strategy

The only question that matters: Can they do this themselves?

"Why would a VP of Sales get on a call with us—and what can we provide to ensure they book AND show?"

Most B2B offers fail because they answer the wrong question. They answer "What sounds valuable?" instead of "What can we give them that they literally cannot get anywhere else?"

A VP of Sales evaluating an offer will ask four questions:

1. Why would I trust you to do this for me?

2. Why would I share my data with a stranger?

3. Why wouldn't I just do this myself?

4. Is this a pitch disguised as value?

Effective offers must pass all four. The only way: offer something they genuinely cannot do themselves—or cannot do at the speed and quality you can deliver.

The Five Offers

Flagship

5 Accounts, 48 Hours

"Give us 5 accounts you're trying to close or break into. In 48 hours, we'll hand you the complete buying committee for each—Champion, Decision Maker, Blockers, Stakeholders—with pain intelligence and communication style for every person. Free. No strings."

What They Get

  • Complete buying committee map per account
  • Classification: Champion / DM / Blocker / Stakeholder
  • Pain intelligence per stakeholder
  • Personal archetype & communication style
  • Influence map: who has power

Why It Creates Pull

  • They cannot do this themselves
  • Output is immediately actionable
  • It's real work, not a pitch
  • No catch—they keep it regardless
Pre-Pipeline

The Account Ambush

"Give us 3 accounts you've been trying to break into. We'll map the buying committee and tell you exactly who to call, what they care about, and how to get in—before you make a single dial."

What They Get

  • Who to call first (and why)
  • Who the actual decision maker is
  • Potential blockers & how to neutralize
  • The pain that will resonate

Best For

  • VPs with thin pipeline needing logos
  • Hungry Comeback archetype
  • Frustrated Fighter archetype
Stalled Pipeline

The Deal Rescue

"Give us 3 deals that have gone dark or stalled. We'll tell you who killed them, who can revive them, and exactly what to say to get them moving again."

What They Get

  • The likely blocker (who killed it)
  • Stakeholders they missed
  • Who can revive it (champion path)
  • Re-engagement strategy

Best For

  • Anxious Elite archetype
  • Stressed Performer archetype
  • Anyone with pipeline that's not moving
Capacity Play

The Rep Multiplier

"Your reps spend 30% of their time researching accounts. Pick one rep's entire territory—we'll map every buying committee in 72 hours. See what happens when they spend 100% of their time selling."

What They Get

  • All accounts in one rep's territory (up to 50)
  • Priority ranking by accessibility
  • "Ready to sell" package per account

Gate

  • Requires 30-min strategy call first
  • Best for enterprise / high-intent
  • Demonstrates full Adrata potential
Competitive Intel

Beat Your Competitor

"Give us 3 deals where you're competing head-to-head. We'll tell you which stakeholders your competitor is probably talking to—and which ones they're missing that you can win."

What They Get

  • Full buying committee map
  • Likely competitor engagement pattern
  • Underserved stakeholders (your opportunity)
  • Winning strategy: who to engage, how

Best For

  • Anyone with active competitive deals
  • Anxious Elite who can't afford to lose
  • Panicking Star needing quick wins

What Fails vs. What Works

Offers That Fail

  • "Let us analyze your pipeline"
  • Diagnosis (they know the problem)
  • Generic insight available elsewhere
  • "Trust us, we're experts"
  • Pitch disguised as value

Offers That Create Pull

  • "We'll DO the work your reps spend weeks on"
  • Solution (they can't do this themselves)
  • Proprietary intel on THEIR accounts
  • "Here's $5,000 worth of work, free"
  • Value that naturally leads to product

Why Show Rates Go Up

These offers inherently drive higher show rates:

Commitment device: They have to provide account names, which creates investment before the call.

Tangible deliverable: They know exactly what they're getting (buying committee maps), not a vague "consultation."

Immediate utility: The output is usable the day they receive it.

No pitch expectation: It's framed as "we'll do work for you," not "we'll tell you about our product."

The Core Principle

Don't offer to think for them. Offer to WORK for them.

Every offer should pass this test: "Is this something they literally cannot do themselves, or cannot do at this speed and quality?" If the answer is no, the offer won't create pull.
