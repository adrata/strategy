The Core Model: Demand vs. Supply

KEY INSIGHT: Nobody wants your product. They want to accomplish something in their life. Your product is supply for their demand. Demand is supply-agnostic—it exists whether or not your product exists. Your job is to find demand where existing supply is inadequate, then build supply that fits better.

THE TENSION THAT CREATES PULL:

  • Calendar: What they're actually doing (current supply/actions)
  • To-Do List: What they really want to accomplish (demand)
  • The Gap: When calendar doesn't fit to-do list = PULL exists

TWO TYPES OF DEMAND TENSION:
1. DOING something that doesn't fit demand (visible on calendar—they hate doing this)
2. NOT DOING something they want to do (invisible—blocked because no good supply exists)

For the theoretical foundation of PULL thinking, see Push, Pull, and PULL and Why Most Sales is Backwards.

Why Now: The 2026 Moment

Before exploring specific demand hypotheses, we need to understand why 2026 is different. Why would VP Sales buy NOW when they haven't before?

The 7 Forces Creating Urgency

Force 1: The Headcount Squeeze

What changed: Boards now expect AI to replace headcount, not augment it. The 2024-2025 layoffs proved sales orgs could do more with less—now that's the baseline expectation.

The pressure: Same quota, 30% fewer people. "Hiring more" is no longer an acceptable answer to a pipeline problem.

Why they buy: Any tool that multiplies the output of existing people is now essential, not optional.

Force 2: The AI SDR Flood

What changed: Everyone adopted AI SDRs simultaneously. Buyer inboxes are now 80% AI-generated spam. Response rates to cold outreach have dropped 60% since 2024.

The pressure: The outbound playbook that worked for 20 years is broken. Volume no longer equals pipeline.

Why they buy: Tools that help cut through noise, identify real intent, or work existing pipeline are more valuable than ever. Pipeline quality > quantity.

Force 3: The "DIY With AI" Competitor

What changed: ChatGPT taught everyone they can build things themselves. Every prospect now considers "we'll just build it internally with AI" as a real option.

The pressure: VP Sales are losing deals not to competitors, but to DIY. Buyers think they don't need vendors anymore.

Why they buy: VP Sales themselves are experiencing this with their own buying decisions. They're trying to "ChatGPT" their way through problems—and hitting walls. This makes them more receptive to specialized solutions that work.

Force 4: The Speed Expectation

What changed: Headlines about AI companies going $0-$20M in months have reset board expectations. "Startup speed" now means something different.

The pressure: Boards expect faster everything: faster ramp, faster pipeline generation, faster deal cycles. Patience has evaporated.

Why they buy: Anything that accelerates time-to-revenue is worth paying for. "We can implement next quarter" is no longer acceptable—they need help NOW.

Force 5: The Tenure Crisis

What changed: Average VP Sales tenure dropped from 26 months (2010) to 17-19 months (2025). The job has never been more precarious.

The pressure: You have 2-3 quarters to prove yourself. There's no runway for "building for the long term." Results must be immediate.

Why they buy: Tools that show fast ROI (90 days or less) will get budget. VP Sales are personally incentivized to find solutions that work quickly—their jobs depend on it.

Force 6: The Data Is Finally There

What changed: 5 years of call recording (Gong, Chorus), CRM data, email tracking, and calendar integration means there's finally enough signal to power real AI insights.

The pressure: "We have all this data but can't use it" is a common complaint. They're paying for tools that capture data but don't deliver actionable insight.

Why they buy: Solutions that synthesize existing data into insight (not just more dashboards) will resonate. The data is there—they need someone to make it useful.

Force 7: The ChatGPT Moment Opened Minds

What changed: ChatGPT's breakthrough created a new mental model: "AI = software that's kinda human-like." This opened buyers' minds to possibilities they couldn't imagine before.

The pressure: VP Sales are now actively asking: "Is there a better way to do X, now that AI exists?" They're seeking solutions, not waiting to be sold.

Why they buy: Demand that was previously blocked ("this is impossible") is now unblocked ("maybe AI can do this"). VP Sales have projects on their to-do list that they now believe can be solved.

The 2026 Summary

VP Sales have:

  • Less headcount
  • Broken outbound playbooks
  • Buyers who think they can DIY
  • Boards expecting AI-company speed
  • 17 months to prove themselves
  • Mountains of unused data
  • Minds open to AI solutions

This is a moment of maximum PULL. The demand exists. The existing supply is failing. They're actively looking for better options.

Demand Hypothesis #1

"I need to know what's real in my pipeline without relying on rep optimism."

The Demand (To-Do List)

"I want to look at my pipeline and instantly know which deals are real and which are wishful thinking—without having to interrogate every rep or sit in every deal review. I want the truth about what's going to close, before I find out the hard way."

This demand exists whether or not any product exists to serve it. It's a fundamental desire of every VP Sales in history.

Current Supply (What They're Doing Now)

Supply Option A: Trust the CRM

Limitation: CRM reflects what reps input, not reality. Reps are incentivized to be optimistic.

Calendar reality: VP spends hours in deal reviews trying to find the truth behind the data.

Supply Option B: Weekly Deal Reviews

Limitation: Only covers subset of deals. Relies on reps self-reporting. Takes 3-5 hours/week.

Calendar reality: VP sits in every review, asks the same questions, still gets surprised.

Supply Option C: Forecast Calls with Managers

Limitation: Managers are also optimistic. Information is already filtered.

Calendar reality: VP re-forecasts constantly because first forecast was wrong.

Supply Option D: AI Tools (Current Generation)

Limitation: Most AI tools just surface data, don't synthesize signal. "More dashboards" isn't the answer.

Calendar reality: VP opens another dashboard, sees more data, still doesn't know what's real.

Supply Option E: DIY with ChatGPT

Limitation: Can't access real-time deal data. No integration. Manual copy-paste.

Calendar reality: VP tries it once, gives up, goes back to intuition.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP spends 5-10 hours/week trying to figure out what's real (calendar) but still doesn't have confidence (to-do list unmet).
Ideal supply: A single source of truth that scores every deal's "realness" based on signals they can't see manually—buyer engagement, stakeholder activity, pattern matching against similar deals that won/lost. VP looks at one number per deal, knows what's real.
Validation question: "How much time do you spend trying to figure out which deals are real? What do you do when you're surprised by a slip?"

Demand Hypothesis #2

"I need to see deals slipping before they slip."

The Demand (To-Do List)

"I want to know a deal is at risk BEFORE it misses its close date—not after. I want early warning so I can intervene, not post-mortems where I learn what went wrong too late to fix it."

Every VP Sales has been blindsided by a deal that "looked fine" until it didn't. The demand for foresight is universal.

Current Supply (What They're Doing Now)

Supply Option A: Rep Self-Reporting

Limitation: Reps report problems late (protecting their pipeline), or don't recognize risk.

Calendar reality: VP finds out about risk when deal is already lost.

Supply Option B: Stage-Based Forecasting

Limitation: A deal can be in "Negotiation" stage for 3 months and still be dead. Stage doesn't equal health.

Calendar reality: VP looks at stage distribution, feels confident, still misses.

Supply Option C: Gut Feel / Experience

Limitation: Works for deals VP is close to. Doesn't scale. Can't monitor 200 deals personally.

Calendar reality: VP focuses on top 10 deals, gets surprised by deal #47.

Supply Option D: Call Recording Analysis

Limitation: Requires listening to calls (time-intensive) or trusting AI summaries that miss nuance.

Calendar reality: VP doesn't have time to listen. Reviews summaries that don't surface risk.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP learns about deal risk reactively, not proactively. By the time it's visible, it's too late.
Ideal supply: Automated early warning system that detects slip signals 30-60 days before the deal misses: declining engagement, champion going quiet, stakeholder changes, competitive mentions. Alert VP to intervene while there's still time.
Validation question: "When was the last time you were surprised by a deal slipping? What would you have done differently if you'd known 30 days earlier?"

Demand Hypothesis #3

"I need to know who's really involved in buying decisions—not just who's in our meetings."

The Demand (To-Do List)

"I want to see the FULL buying committee—including the people who have influence but never show up in our meetings. The IT person who will veto on security. The CFO's analyst who will kill the deal on price. The exec whose pet project competes for the same budget. I want to find them before they find us."

Complex B2B deals have 6-10 stakeholders on average. Reps typically know 2-3. The hidden ones kill deals.

Current Supply (What They're Doing Now)

Supply Option A: Ask the Champion

Limitation: Champion may not know everyone involved. May not want to admit they don't have full access.

Calendar reality: Rep asks, champion says "I've got it covered," deal stalls in procurement.

Supply Option B: Org Chart Research

Limitation: Static org charts don't show influence. The CIO's title looks important but their direct report makes the real decision.

Calendar reality: Rep researches org chart, targets wrong people.

Supply Option C: Multi-Threading Training

Limitation: Reps know they SHOULD multi-thread. They don't know WHO to thread to.

Calendar reality: Rep adds more people to emails, doesn't add the right people.

Supply Option D: LinkedIn Sales Navigator

Limitation: Shows who's at the company, not who's involved in THIS deal.

Calendar reality: Rep has a list of 50 people, no idea which 5 matter.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP and reps can't see who they're NOT talking to. The hidden stakeholders are invisible until they veto.
Ideal supply: AI that detects likely buying committee members from email patterns, meeting invites, org data, and similar closed-won deals. Surface: "For deals like this at companies like theirs, these roles are typically involved—and you haven't engaged them yet."
Validation question: "In your last lost deal, were there people involved that you didn't know about until it was too late? How did you find out?"

Demand Hypothesis #4

"I need to present pipeline to the board without looking like I don't know what's going on."

The Demand (To-Do List)

"I want to walk into a board meeting with confidence that my forecast is right. I want to answer questions without hedging. I want the board to trust my numbers—because I trust my numbers."

The board meeting is the highest-stakes moment for a VP Sales. Getting it wrong—especially missing after committing—destroys credibility.

Current Supply (What They're Doing Now)

Supply Option A: Over-Engineer the Forecast

Limitation: More spreadsheet complexity doesn't mean more accuracy. Garbage in, garbage out.

Calendar reality: VP spends days building elaborate models that still miss.

Supply Option B: Sandbag Everything

Limitation: Consistent under-promising protects credibility short-term, but creates its own problems.

Calendar reality: VP commits to 80% of what they think they can hit, lives in fear of upside questions.

Supply Option C: Trust the Team's Commit

Limitation: Roll-up forecasts are systemically optimistic. 10 reps slightly over-commit = big miss.

Calendar reality: VP presents what reps committed, then scrambles to explain the miss.

Supply Option D: Historical Win Rate Math

Limitation: Backward-looking averages don't account for current deal health.

Calendar reality: VP applies 30% win rate to pipeline, but this quarter's pipeline is healthier/weaker than average.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP can't confidently forecast because they don't have a reliable signal for deal health. Every method they use has systematic flaws.
Ideal supply: AI-powered forecast that weighs deal-specific health signals, not just stage and close date. VP presents forecast with confidence interval: "We have 90% confidence we'll hit $2.3M, 60% confidence we'll hit $2.8M. Here's why."
Validation question: "What's your process for getting to a forecast number before a board meeting? How confident are you when you present it?"

Demand Hypothesis #5

"I need to know what's actually happening in deals without sitting in every call."

The Demand (To-Do List)

"I want to know what's REALLY happening in deals—what buyers are saying, what objections are coming up, what competitors are being mentioned—without having to listen to every call or read every email. I want the signal without the noise."

VP Sales need to know what's happening at the ground level, but they can't scale their time. This is blocked demand—they want it but can't do it.

Current Supply (What They're Doing Now)

Supply Option A: Join Calls Personally

Limitation: Doesn't scale. Can only be in one place at a time. Changes rep behavior when VP is on the call.

Calendar reality: VP joins 2-3 calls/week, misses 50+ others.

Supply Option B: Read Call Summaries (Gong, etc.)

Limitation: Summaries miss nuance. Don't surface what you didn't know to look for.

Calendar reality: VP skims summaries, misses critical signals buried in the noise.

Supply Option C: Ask Reps What Happened

Limitation: Reps filter and spin. They emphasize what went well, downplay concerns.

Calendar reality: VP asks, rep says "it went great," deal dies two weeks later.

Supply Option D: Wait for Deal Review

Limitation: Weekly cadence is too slow. A lot can happen in 7 days.

Calendar reality: VP learns about problem in Thursday deal review, deal already slipped on Tuesday.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP wants omniscience (know everything) but has finite time. Current supply gives either too much data (overwhelming) or too little (filtered).
Ideal supply: AI that surfaces only the signals that matter: "This deal mentioned [competitor] for the first time." "Champion was defensive when asked about timeline." "New stakeholder appeared who asked hard pricing questions." Not summaries—alerts.
Validation question: "How do you stay informed about what's happening in your deals? What do you miss that you wish you caught earlier?"

Demand Hypothesis #6

"I need to get my team to hit number without burning them out."

The Demand (To-Do List)

"I want my team to hit their numbers—but I also want them to still be here next year. I want sustainable performance, not heroics that lead to turnover. I want to know when someone is struggling before they give notice."

70% of sales reps report burnout. Turnover is expensive. VP Sales need output AND retention—both at once.

Current Supply (What They're Doing Now)

Supply Option A: Push Harder

Limitation: Works short-term, destroys team long-term. Best reps leave first.

Calendar reality: VP pushes, team burns out, turnover creates more pressure.

Supply Option B: Regular 1:1s

Limitation: Reps don't always share struggles. By the time they do, it's often too late.

Calendar reality: VP has good 1:1, thinks rep is fine, rep gives notice two weeks later.

Supply Option C: Hire More

Limitation: Boards aren't approving headcount. Ramp time means new hires don't help this quarter.

Calendar reality: VP requests headcount, gets rejected, back to same problem.

Supply Option D: Work Smarter Platitudes

Limitation: Without specific tools or process changes, "work smarter" is just words.

Calendar reality: VP says "prioritize," team doesn't know HOW to prioritize.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP can't see inside reps' heads to know who's struggling, and can't magically create more capacity.
Ideal supply: Tools that (1) eliminate low-value work so reps have more time for selling, and (2) surface early warning signals when reps are disengaged or struggling. Help VP do more with same people, while protecting those people.
Validation question: "How do you balance hitting number with keeping your team healthy? Have you lost anyone recently that you wish you'd seen coming?"

Demand Hypothesis #7

"I need to prove to leadership that it's a pipeline quality problem, not a closing problem."

The Demand (To-Do List)

"I know the problem isn't my team's ability to close. It's the quality of what's coming in—leads that aren't ready, deals that shouldn't be in pipeline, opportunities without real PULL. But I can't prove it. I look like I'm making excuses."

VP Sales often know the truth but can't demonstrate it with data. They're blamed for symptoms of upstream problems.

Current Supply (What They're Doing Now)

Supply Option A: Anecdotal Arguments

Limitation: "Trust me" doesn't work with CEOs and boards. Sounds like blame-shifting.

Calendar reality: VP says "leads are bad," marketing says "sales can't close," stalemate.

Supply Option B: Simple Conversion Metrics

Limitation: "Lead-to-opportunity conversion" doesn't tell the full story. Deals can be created but still be bad.

Calendar reality: VP shows numbers, CEO says "but your pipeline is full."

Supply Option C: Win/Loss Analysis

Limitation: Win/loss is backward-looking and subjective. Reps blame product, marketing, price.

Calendar reality: VP does win/loss reviews, gets inconclusive results, nothing changes.

Supply Option D: Ask for Better Leads

Limitation: Without data, this is just complaining. Marketing has their own metrics showing leads are fine.

Calendar reality: Sales vs. marketing finger-pointing continues. Nobody wins.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP needs EVIDENCE that pipeline quality is the issue—not opinions, not finger-pointing, DATA.
Ideal supply: Analytics that score pipeline quality at creation time, not just at close time. Show: "67% of MQLs that became opportunities had no budget authority. 48% of pipeline had no compelling event. Here's the source breakdown." Turn opinion into evidence.
Validation question: "When you miss, how do you explain it to leadership? Do you feel like you can prove what's really happening, or does it sound like excuses?"

Demand Hypothesis #8

"I need to onboard and ramp new reps faster than ever."

The Demand (To-Do List)

"I need new hires producing revenue in months, not quarters. I don't have the runway for a 6-month ramp. Every week a rep isn't selling is quota I'm missing. I need them ready NOW."

With shorter VP tenures and higher board expectations, the traditional 6-month ramp window is gone.

Current Supply (What They're Doing Now)

Supply Option A: Sales Playbook / Wiki

Limitation: Static documents go stale. Reps don't read them. Doesn't help in live situations.

Calendar reality: Rep skims playbook Day 1, never opens it again, makes same mistakes as predecessors.

Supply Option B: Shadow Top Performers

Limitation: Top performers are busy selling, not training. Shadowing is passive, not active learning.

Calendar reality: New rep shadows, watches but doesn't understand WHY things work.

Supply Option C: Sales Enablement Training

Limitation: Generic training doesn't address company-specific knowledge. Classroom ≠ field.

Calendar reality: Rep completes training, scores well on quiz, fails on first live call.

Supply Option D: Trial by Fire

Limitation: Some learn, many fail. High early attrition. Expensive mistakes on real deals.

Calendar reality: New rep burns leads while learning, makes mistakes customers remember.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: Traditional ramp takes 6 months. VP needs it in 3. Current tools don't accelerate learning from doing.
Ideal supply: AI that acts as a real-time coach during calls (not after), surfaces relevant content at moment of need, and benchmarks new rep behavior against top performers. Show rep exactly what to say/do in live situations, not generic training.
Validation question: "How long does it take a new rep to ramp? What would change if you could cut that in half?"

"I need outbound that actually works in 2026."

The Demand (To-Do List)

"I need to generate pipeline from outbound. But the old playbook doesn't work anymore. Response rates are in the toilet. Buyers ignore everything that smells like automation. I need a way to reach people that doesn't get deleted instantly."

The AI SDR flood destroyed traditional outbound. But VP Sales still need pipeline. Demand unchanged, supply broken.

Current Supply (What They're Doing Now)

Supply Option A: AI SDRs (Automated Outbound)

Limitation: Everyone has them. Buyers can detect AI instantly. Response rates approaching zero.

Calendar reality: AI SDR sends 1000 emails, gets 2 meetings, both are terrible fit.

Supply Option B: Human SDRs (Old Model)

Limitation: Expensive. High turnover. Can't match AI volume. Still using same tired messaging.

Calendar reality: SDR team burns through lists, meetings booked aren't quality.

Supply Option C: Inbound Dependence

Limitation: Not enough inbound to hit targets. Marketing can't generate enough demand.

Calendar reality: VP waits for inbound, comes up short, scrambles for outbound.

Supply Option D: "Personal" Outreach at Scale

Limitation: Personalization that's templated still feels fake. Buyers see through it.

Calendar reality: Rep sends "personalized" email, buyer sees through it, deletes.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: Old outbound is dead. New outbound hasn't been figured out yet. VP needs pipeline now.
Ideal supply: Tools that identify REAL buyer intent signals (not just firmographic fit). People who are actually researching, comparing, or showing buying behavior. Quality over quantity. Also: ways to stand out as human in an AI-flooded inbox.
Validation question: "How's your outbound performing compared to 2 years ago? What's working now that didn't work before—or vice versa?"

Demand Hypothesis #10

"I need to figure out what AI tools to actually adopt—and which are noise."

The Demand (To-Do List)

"I'm drowning in AI sales tools. Everyone promises the world. I don't have time to evaluate 50 vendors. I need to know which ones actually work, which ones are hype, and in what order to adopt them. I need a shortcut through the noise."

The explosion of AI tools has created its own problem: paralysis. VP Sales know they should be using AI, but don't know where to start.

Current Supply (What They're Doing Now)

Supply Option A: Read Reviews / G2 / Peer Recommendations

Limitation: Reviews are gamed. Peers have different contexts. Still requires evaluation.

Calendar reality: VP reads 10 G2 reviews, all say "great tool," still doesn't know.

Supply Option B: Vendor Demos

Limitation: Time-consuming. Every vendor looks great in their own demo. Can't compare apples to apples.

Calendar reality: VP takes 15 demos, more confused than when started.

Supply Option C: Hire RevOps to Figure It Out

Limitation: RevOps overwhelmed. They're already managing existing stack.

Calendar reality: RevOps is underwater, AI evaluation becomes "next quarter."

Supply Option D: Adopt Everything

Limitation: Tool fatigue. Reps won't use multiple tools. Integration nightmares.

Calendar reality: VP buys 5 tools, team uses 1.5, rest becomes shelfware.

THE PULL OPPORTUNITY
Gap: VP knows they need AI but can't navigate the landscape. Too many options, not enough time.
Ideal supply: (1) Curated, honest guidance on what works (not vendor-sponsored). (2) A tool that does ONE thing extremely well—so it's obvious where it fits—rather than trying to be a platform. (3) Clear ROI story with fast time-to-value.
Validation question: "How are you deciding what AI tools to adopt? What's your process for evaluating what's real vs. hype?"

Synthesis: The Core Patterns

PATTERN 1: VISIBILITY GAPS
Multiple hypotheses point to the same root issue: VP Sales can't SEE what they need to see.
  • Can't see which deals are real (#1)
  • Can't see deals slipping (#2)
  • Can't see hidden stakeholders (#3)
  • Can't see what's happening in calls (#5)
  • Can't see pipeline quality (#7)

The underlying demand: "I want to see the truth about my business—not filtered through rep optimism or buried in dashboards."

PATTERN 2: TIME COMPRESSION
Multiple hypotheses point to pressure to move faster.
  • Need faster ramp (#8)
  • Need to catch problems earlier (#2)
  • Need outbound that converts now (#9)
  • Need to prove value to boards fast (#4)

The underlying demand: "I need results faster than traditional methods allow—because I don't have time to wait."

PATTERN 3: CREDIBILITY & PROOF
Multiple hypotheses point to needing evidence, not opinions.
  • Need to forecast with confidence (#4)
  • Need to prove pipeline quality issues (#7)
  • Need to demonstrate control to board

The underlying demand: "I need data that makes me credible—so when I say something, people believe me."

PATTERN 4: SCALE WITHOUT DESTRUCTION
Multiple hypotheses point to tension between output and sustainability.
  • Need to hit number without burnout (#6)
  • Need reps to perform without handholding
  • Need pipeline without massive SDR team

The underlying demand: "I need to multiply output without multiplying headcount or burning people out."

The Thesis: Why Now, Why Us

The 2026 Opportunity Statement

VP Sales have always wanted to see the truth about their pipeline. Their existing supply (CRM data, deal reviews, gut feel) has always been inadequate.

But in 2026, three things have changed:

  1. THE DATA EXISTS: 5 years of call recordings, emails, and CRM history mean there's finally enough signal to power real insight.
  2. THE AI CAN INTERPRET IT: LLMs can now synthesize unstructured data (calls, emails) into actionable intelligence—not just dashboards.
  3. THE PRESSURE IS EXISTENTIAL: 17-month tenure, smaller teams, broken outbound, speed expectations—VP Sales can't afford to guess anymore.

The demand has always existed. The supply has never been good enough. AI has changed the supply equation. This is the moment.

The demand is for truth and foresight. AI is just how we deliver it.
