K > 1.0 = Viral (each user brings more than one new user)
Target: 25% invite rate (1 in 4 users sends an invite)
Target: 40% accept rate (invitees who become users)

K = invite rate × accept rate × users per invite

Why VP Sales Is Different

THE CRISIS: Average VP Sales tenure is 17-19 months. Only 43% of reps hit quota. 70% experience burnout. First 90 days determine survival.

THE OPPORTUNITY: VP Sales are in survival mode. They share what helps them survive. Growth tactics must feel like GAINING insight, not being marketed to.

Section 1: Viral Loops

PSYCHOLOGY: Viral loops exploit Reciprocity (people return favors), Social Proof (others doing it validates action), and Loss Aversion (don't lose what you could have).

1A. Referral Mechanics

Personal Referral Links
Every user gets unique URL. Track who brings whom. Show count on dashboard. Use Goal Gradient—show 'X referrals to next reward' to accelerate invites.
Two-Sided Rewards
Both referrer and referee get something. 'Your colleague gets 20% off, you get Pipeline Score unlock.' Exploits Reciprocity—they're GIVING something.
Invite-to-Unlock Features
Free tier gets 3 diagnostics. Invite 2 colleagues = unlock 5 more. Creates Partial Unlock psychology—they've started, need to finish.
Tiered Referral Rewards
1 referral = feature. 5 = free month. 10 = Ambassador status with exclusive badge. Pseudo-Set Framing—they want to complete the set.
Time-Limited Bonuses
'Invite this week, both get double rewards.' Creates Scarcity urgency. Best for Panicking Star and Sweating Target archetypes.
Referral Leaderboard
Monthly top 10 referrers get recognition. Variable Rewards—not everyone wins, but everyone could. Hawthorne Effect—public visibility changes behavior.
Cash Referral Bonuses
$50-100 per qualified referral. PayPal paid $10. High LTV justifies it. Works best for Stressed Performer (needs win) and Hungry Underdog (needs money).
Exclusive Access Rewards
Refer 3 = early access to new features. Creates exclusivity that Anxious Elite and Worried Darling crave.
Waitlist Jump
'Move from #847 to #12 by referring 3 friends.' Combines Scarcity + Endowed Progress + Goal Gradient. Powerful compound.
Referral Milestones with Swag
Physical rewards at 5/10/25 referrals. People photograph and share. IKEA Effect—they earned it, they value it.

1B. Team Spread Mechanics

Team Workspace Invites
One creates workspace → invites team → some leave company → start new workspaces elsewhere. Network effect compounds. The Rebuilding Leader brings tools to every new role.
Manager Seat Triggers
When VP joins, prompt: 'Invite your 3 top performers to see team insights.' Commitment & Consistency—once they've invited one, they'll invite more.
Team Leaderboards
Public performance boards. 'Your team vs industry.' Social Proof + Competitive drive. Managers want all reps visible.
Deal Handoff Invites
When deal moves to CS, auto-invite them. When SDR qualifies for AE, invite AE. Spreads internally through workflow.
Job Change Detection
Reps change jobs every 18-24 months (VP Sales even faster at 17-19). Detect LinkedIn changes and reach out: 'Bring your Pipeline Score to your new role!'
Cross-Department Invites
'Share your win with Marketing.' When Frustrated Fighter proves Marketing Ghost was real, they'll share with Marketing to make the point.

1C. Network Effect Mechanics

Collaborative Deal Rooms
Shared spaces for multi-stakeholder deals. Prospects experience product before buying. Every deal = mini product demo.
Cross-Company Benchmark Networks
'See how 847 VPs at your stage perform.' Network becomes valuable because others are in it. Peer Comparison psychology.
Partner Portal
Agencies and consultants get partner accounts. One partner = many customer introductions. Revenue share creates aligned incentives.
Integration Marketplace
List on HubSpot/Salesforce/Outreach marketplaces. Users discover through existing ecosystem. Low friction adoption.
Alumni Networks
Track users as they move companies. Build community of 'graduates.' Fresh Start Effect—new job = new tools = reactivation opportunity.

Section 2: Shareable Outputs

PSYCHOLOGY: Shareables exploit IKEA Effect (they co-created it, they value it), Peak-End Rule (memorable moment), and Status Signaling (sharing wins signals success). Every share is free advertising.

2A. Diagnostic & Assessment Outputs

Pipeline Exposure Score Card
Beautiful visual showing their score vs benchmark. 'Powered by [Brand]' footer. Designed for LinkedIn/Slack sharing. Singularity Effect—it's THEIR score.
Stakeholder Map Visualization
Beautiful diagram of their deal's stakeholder network. So useful they'll share with team. Recipients ask 'what tool made this?'
Gap Analysis PDF
Professional 5-page report with their inputs, benchmark comparisons, recommendations. Labor Illusion—looks like $5K consulting work.
Maturity Assessment Results
'Your Sales Process Maturity: Level 3 of 5.' Progress bar visual. Zeigarnik Effect—incomplete status motivates action AND sharing.
Personalized Benchmark Report
'You vs 500+ VPs at your stage.' Peer Comparison is the #1 thing executives want. They'll share to show they participated.

2B. Win & Achievement Outputs

Win Celebration Cards
Auto-generate 'I closed a $X deal!' cards for LinkedIn when deals close. One-click share. Micro-Interaction delight at peak moment.
Weekly Stats Cards
'47 calls, 12 meetings, $120K pipeline added' — shareable image like Spotify Wrapped. Use Chunking—3 key metrics, not 10.
Year in Review
Annual summary of user's activity. People will share this if designed well. Peak-End Rule—make it a celebration.
Quarter in Review
Same as annual but quarterly. Fresh Start Effect—tied to Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 natural breakpoints.
Achievement Badges
'100 Deals Analyzed' — shareable badges optimized for LinkedIn. Pseudo-Set Framing—collect the full set.
Streak Celebrations
'7-day active streak!' — shareable achievements. Duolingo proved this works. Variable Rewards—different streaks unlock different visuals.
Goal Achievement Cards
'Hit quota 3 weeks early!' — auto-generated when goals met. The Panicking Star NEEDS to share this to prove Glory Hangover is beaten.
Comeback Story Cards
'From 60% to 110% in 8 weeks.' Narrative Arc—the struggle-to-success story. Rebuilding Leader's dream share.

2C. Competitive & Benchmark Outputs

Benchmark Comparisons
'Top 10% of sales reps for call volume' — shareable stats. Status signaling at its purest.
Industry Ranking Cards
'#47 of 3,200 SaaS Sales Leaders.' Specific numbers create credibility. Anchoring Bias—big number first.
Team vs Industry Reports
Managers share to recruit—'My team outperforms 80% of industry.' Becomes recruiting tool.
Improvement Trajectory
'Moved from bottom 40% to top 20% in 90 days.' Story Arc visualization. The journey is the shareable content.

2D. Document & Deliverable Outputs

AI-Generated Proposals
Beautiful docs with professional design. Recipients ask 'what tool is this?' Every proposal is a product demo.
Meeting Prep Briefs
One-page stakeholder summaries. So useful they forward to team. 'Powered by' footer on every page.
Embeddable Widgets
Buyer group visualizations for presentations. Every deck at every meeting = brand exposure.
Email Signature Badges
'I use [Brand]' badge for signatures. Every email is an ad. Commitment & Consistency—public declaration.
AI Insight Snippets
Tweetable insights: '[Brand] told me the CFO has authority over this decision.' Aha moments designed for sharing.

Section 4: Social Proof & Status

PSYCHOLOGY: Social Proof (others doing it validates action), Authority Bias (experts endorse it), and Status Seeking (exclusivity signals success). VP Sales are competitive—they want what other VP Sales have.

4A. Certification & Recognition

Professional Certification
Free certification program. Users get LinkedIn badge. 'Certified [Methodology] Expert.' Creates ambassadors who publicly identify with brand.
Power User Status
'Top 1% of users' badges. Exclusivity that Anxious Elite and Worried Darling crave. They'll share because it signals competence.
Customer Advisory Board
Top users get exclusive access and influence. They champion to their network. Authority Bias—'I advise [Brand]' carries weight.
Early Adopter Recognition
'Founding Member' permanent badge. People love being first. Never expires. IKEA Effect—they were part of building it.
Skills Endorsements
'Pipeline Expert' badges based on usage. Designed for LinkedIn profiles. Every profile view = brand impression.

4B. Case Studies & Spotlights

Customer Spotlight
Feature users on blog/podcast. They share content widely. Singularity Effect—it's about THEM specifically.
Case Study Co-Creation
Create with customers, not about them. They co-own it. IKEA Effect—they helped build it, they'll share it.
Video Testimonials
Short clips users can share. 'How I shortened my cycle 40%.' Narrative Arc—problem → solution → result.
Turnaround Stories
'From 60% to 120% in one quarter.' The Rebuilding Leader's favorite content. They share to show what's possible.
Annual Awards
'Sales Leader of the Year' awards. Winners share everywhere. Creates aspiration for non-winners.

4C. Exclusive Communities

Slack/Discord Community
Power users help each other, share tips. Organic recruitment. Social Proof—'847 VP Sales are in here.'
Private Peer Groups
'Series B VP Sales Mastermind.' Exclusivity by stage. Worried Darling wants private, confidential spaces.
Regional Chapters
'NYC Sales Leaders.' Geography creates intimacy. In-person events drive belonging.
Industry Verticals
'Fintech Sales Leaders.' Niche = deep relevance. Familiarity Bias—'these are my people.'

Section 6: Gamification

PSYCHOLOGY: Variable Rewards (unpredictability creates obsession), Goal Gradient (acceleration toward goal), Zeigarnik Effect (incomplete tasks remembered), and Learning Loop (fun = learning). Gamification isn't points—it's the psychological mechanics that make games engaging.

6A. Progress & Streaks

Daily Streaks
Duolingo-style. '15-day streak!' Loss Aversion—don't break the streak. Variable Rewards—different streak milestones unlock different rewards.
XP and Levels
Earn XP for activities. Level up unlocks features. Endowed Progress—they've already started.
Achievement System
'First $100K deal', '100 calls in a week' — shareable badges. Pseudo-Set Framing—collect them all.
Progress Rings
Apple Watch-style daily goals. Visual and satisfying. Close the ring. Zeigarnik Effect—incomplete ring bothers them.
Mastery Trees
Skill progression visualization. 'Pipeline Master' → 'Stakeholder Expert' → 'Deal Architect.' Learning Loop made visible.

6B. Competition & Leaderboards

Sales Challenges
Weekly challenges. Winners share, losers recruit friends to help. Time-boxed competitions create urgency.
Competitive Leaderboards
Company and industry rankings. Top performers get recognition. Hawthorne Effect—public visibility changes behavior.
Team Competitions
Company A vs B. Teams recruit more users to win. Network effect through competition.
Seasonal Tournaments
Quarterly championships. Fresh Start Effect—new quarter, new chance.
Head-to-Head Matches
Challenge specific peers. Personal stakes increase engagement.

6C. Rewards & Surprises

Random Reward Drops
Surprise bonuses. Variable Rewards—unpredictability increases engagement. 'Mystery insight' after random actions.
Milestone Swag
Physical rewards at milestones. People photograph and share → organic marketing.
Feature Unlocks
Use = earn. The more they use, the more they unlock. Progressive Disclosure through gamification.
Loot Box Mechanics
Complete action → random reward type (insight, badge, feature, discount). Variable Rewards psychology.
Daily Spin
Log in → spin wheel for reward. Creates daily habit. Ritual behavior.

Section 9: Quick Wins

PSYCHOLOGY: Micro-Interactions (small moments of delight), Pacing (one thing at a time), and Hintiness (subtle cues, not interruptions). These are low-effort, high-impact optimizations. Implement in days, not weeks.

9A. Share Optimization

Add Share Buttons Everywhere
Every report, achievement, win — one-click share to LinkedIn/Twitter. Reduce friction to zero.
Social Meta Tags
Beautiful Open Graph images for all shareable links. Control how content appears when shared.
Pre-Written Share Copy
Draft the tweet/post for them. They just click. Remove cognitive load from sharing.
Screenshot Optimization
Design key screens to look great when screenshotted. People will screenshot and share organically.

9B. Referral Optimization

Show Referral Link in Onboarding
Plant the seed early. 'Your personal link: xyz.' Endowed Progress—they already have something.
Referral Tracking Dashboard
Show who they've referred and status. Gamifies referrals. Goal Gradient—'2 more for next reward.'
'Invite Team' Prompt After First Win
'Want to invite your team to see this?' Peak moment = best time to ask.
NPS Survey → Share
After positive NPS: 'Great! Share with a colleague?' Convert satisfaction into action.

9C. Delight Optimization

Confetti on Milestones
Celebrate with animation + share prompt. People screenshot confetti. Peak-End Rule.
Sound on Achievements
Satisfying audio feedback. Sensory Appeal—multi-sense = memorable.
Personalized Celebrations
'Great job, [Name]! That's your 5th win this month.' Singularity Effect.
Easter Eggs
Hidden features for power users to discover. Variable Rewards—unexpected delight.

The Growth Equation

Growth = (Value Delivered × Shareability × Triggers) / Friction

Every tactic should: (1) Deliver real value, (2) Be designed for sharing, (3) Appear at peak moments, (4) Have zero-friction execution.

REMEMBER: VP Sales are in survival mode. They share what helps them survive. Make your product so valuable that NOT sharing feels like withholding help from peers.
